This page explains how Speak Solve Alarm handles user information. The Japanese version is the canonical version.

1. Scope

This policy applies to information handled in connection with Speak Solve Alarm features such as alarms, timers, stopwatch, world clock, weather, ads, billing, and optional social posting integrations.

2. Information We Collect or Handle

  • Settings required for app features such as alarms, timers, world clock, weather display, stop tasks, and social posting
  • Purchase status and product information required for Google Play Billing
  • Identifiers and usage information required for ad delivery and measurement through AdMob
  • If Mastodon / Misskey integration is used: instance information, account information, access tokens, posting targets, and posting templates
  • City names, search terms, and region information required to retrieve weather data through Open-Meteo
  • Settings and status information required for notifications, overlay display, and full-screen display

3. Purpose of Use

  • To provide app functions such as alarms, timers, notifications, screen display, and speech output
  • To provide optional functions such as ads, billing confirmation, social posting, and weather retrieval
  • To investigate defects and improve quality and usability

4. Stored Data

Some data, such as app settings, alarm information, world clock settings, stop task settings, and social posting settings, may be stored on the device or by linked services. Social integration data is handled only when the user enables the integration.

5. Third-Party Services

  • Google AdMob (ad delivery)
  • Google Play Billing (in-app purchases)
  • Mastodon / Misskey (servers chosen by the user)
  • Open-Meteo (weather retrieval)

Information handled by these services is subject to the policies of each provider or the user-selected server.

6. Permissions and Display Features

The app may use notifications, overlay display, and full-screen display to make alarms and timers function properly. These features are used to provide alerts, on-screen controls, and user interaction flows.

7. Sharing with Third Parties

Except where required by law, we do not sell user information. However, information may be sent to third-party services when necessary to provide features such as ad delivery, billing verification, social posting, and weather retrieval.
